SWAP

Duration : 36 Hours Virtual Hackathon
Strategies : User Flow, Prototyping, UX/UI Design, Branding
Team : Abby Lui, Sammi Yeung, Grace Tse, Kritika Gupta

INTRODUCTION

Swap is a mobile application that allows users to switch daily routines with friends, groups, or strangers to exchange their daily schedules. The app allows users to add friends, add photos for every event, categorize the routine based on certain moods, and choose to either randomize swap or to select specific people or groups to swap with. ​ As the only designer on the team, I had the opportunity to perform all phases of analysis, design, prototyping, and user testing.


THE PROBLEM

"How might we make mundane life more fun?"

“...experiencing new and diverse experiences on a daily basis is linked to positive emotions”

- Aaron Heller

“Cognitive and social engagement have been shown to be protective against cognitive decline[,]... hearing loss, depression, and social isolation”

- Dr. Kathryn Papp

IDEATION

My team and I came up with ideas on some features we could include in our app. I organized our ideas and evaluated which ones would suit the user demographic and benefit the main goal of our project. From here, I created a user flowchart to map out what our app would incorporate as well as the user's journey through our product.


WIREFRAMING

From here, I started to create a basic wireframe of the flow


DESIGN

I started off my designs by a colour palette and mood board based on the theme of our project. As the goal was to make daily life fun, I chose a pleasant and cheerful colour palette.



From here, I created basic designs for the four pages on the menu. This included the home page, the search pages, the friends and requests page, and the personal daily routine page. Parts of the page were laid out based on the user flow chart and for a user-friendly experience. On the top of my homepage is the logo I developed, which is supposed to resemble the letter "S" from the word "Swap"


PROTOTYPE

With all of the pages I created, I developed a simple prototype to demonstrate how the user would maneuver through our application.


FINAL

Since this was my first Hackathon and I was the only designer for the team, this was definitely a fun learning experience. One thing that I was not able to fix due to the time constraints was adding an additional button on the personal routine page to view the days that are free and the days that are booked for swaps. Although users could view this page already when they are scheduling their swaps, the weekly schedule should be available for them to view at all times. Overall, this experience allowed me to practice being focused on prioritizing the problem we were trying to solve and being open-minded about all the concepts we came up with. I definitely enjoyed working with my team and I can't wait to design more projects during future Hackathons.
