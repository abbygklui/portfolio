About this Project


Swap is a mobile application that allows users to switch daily routines with friends, groups, or strangers to exchange their daily schedules. The app allows users to add friends, add photos for every event, categorize the routine based on certain moods, and choose to either randomize swap or to select specific people or groups to swap with.â€‹ As the only designer on the team, I had the opportunity to perform all phases of analysis, design, prototyping, and user testing.

Problem

Young adults experience boredom in their lives regularly

Boredom has been on the rise for early adolescents since 2010. This is connected to the fact that young adults are given more freedom to choose what to do everyday, but are still in the stage of learning about themselves and what interest them. This increase in freedom also includes daily responsibilites that can be considered as dull and repetative. The loss of routine that was developed since they were young, replaced by mundane responsibilites from freedom can lead to boredom.

"How can we offer a product that makes mundane life more fun?"

Our Approach


As most adolescents have difficulty choosing their interests and daily activities, leading to boredom and negative emotions, we directed our platform to offer user-personalized daily routines. people can swap daily routines with their friends, or users all around the world. They can try out new and different activies according to the schedule and add/view photos posted of users completing each activities.

Design

Ideation

My team and I came up with ideas on some features we could include in our app. I organized our ideas and evaluated which ones would suit the user demographic and benefit the main goal of our project.

From here, I created a user flowchart to map out what our app would incorporate as well as the user's journey through our product.

Wireframing


Based on the flow and our finalized features, I created a low lo-fidelity wireframe

Branding


I then created a colour palette and mood board based on the theme of our project. As the goal was to make the daily lives of adolescents fun, I chose a pleasant and cheerful colour palette.

From here, I created basic designs for the four pages on the menu. This included the home page, the search pages, the friends and requests page, and the personal daily routine page. Parts of the page were laid out based on the user flow chart and for a user-friendly experience. On the top of my homepage is the logo I developed, which is supposed to resemble the letter "S" from the word "Swap"

I then coordinated with my team to see if there were any changes I should make before continuing

The Final


With all of the pages I created, I developed a simple prototype to demonstrate how the user would maneuver through our application.

My Reflection


Since this was my first Hackathon and I was the only designer for the team, this was definitely a fun learning experience. One thing that I was not able to fix due to the time constraints was adding an additional button on the personal routine page to view the days that are free and the days that are booked for swaps. Although users could view this page already when they are scheduling their swaps, the weekly schedule should be available for them to view at all times. Overall, this experience allowed me to practice being focused on prioritizing the problem we were trying to solve and being open-minded about all the concepts we came up with. I definitely enjoyed working with my team and I can't wait to design more projects during future Hackathons.
