Quanne

Book and Editorial Typeface Design

Duration : 8 Weeks

Type : Personal Project

Responsibilities : Typeface Design, Layout Design, Typography

Tools : InDesign, Illustrator, Glyphs

About this Project


Quanne is a contemporary compact serif typeface designed for intensive book and editorial use. Its tone is both practical and classical, asserting credibility while sustaining comfortable reading. Its relaxed ambiance brings in a trace of human touch.

Problem

Today, many serif typefaces use thin hairlines and short serifs that disappear in smaller sizes or when printed in poor quality and low resolution.

Quanne uses a low-stroke contrast to solve this problem.


Each letterform is strong, open, and subtle for clarity when used for passages of text.

Characters are well-defined with open apertures and large counters. While the design keeps a variety of glyphs to form clearer word images, curves and shapes remain very much alike, resulting in a consistent and harmonious look from afar.

Reflection


During this project, I realized that typeface creation is an ongoing process that we can always improve upon after reflection and constantly receiving feedback. Every time I opened my laptop, I noticed identified new areas for improvement.

As I reflect further on my experience in typeface design, I realize that there is always room for growth and improvement. Although I am proud of my progress, I know that next time I revisit this project, I will notice additional areas that could benefit from more attention and refinement.

Overall, I enjoyed working on this project and I learned valuable lessons in problem-solving and empathy towards the end-users of my designs. As this was my first time diving deep into typeface designs, I gained confidence in creating vector fonts using Illustrator and InDesign. I'm proud of my ability to enhance readability through lettering.
