Global Consumerism

Poster Series

Duration : 8 Weeks

Type : School Project

Responsibilities : Layout Design, Typography, Data Visualization

Tools : InDesign, Illustrator

About this Project


For this project, I was asked to create a series of posters through research content and data visualization for my target audience. This typographic and data visualization series should come together to tell a story about an environmental issue.

Definition of Issue

Consumerism is a social ideology that encourages mindless consumption and purchases of non-essential goods. Today, this excessive mindset has spurred the rise of consumer culture in both developed and developing countries. Consumerism has become part of what drives society, promoting fast fashion and products designed with planned obsolescence in mind. This, in turn, has created a depletion of resources, pollution, and waste through the continuous life cycles of the purchases. This capitalist system is taking a large toll on the environment as one of the key drivers the acceleration of climate change.

Objective/Aim


Through this project, I aim to understand the different environmental factors that are negatively impacted by consumerism and where the problems lie in the cycle of consumerism. I want to show what needs to be changed for the cycle to become less harmful.

Learning Outcomes


Through the completion of this project, I will be able to grow my skills in these focus areas.

  1. Justify and apply the use of theoretical and practical knowledge employing various methods for the visual representation of data.

  2. Use discipline-specific terminology related to visualization methods and approaches in visual and verbal communication.

  3. Gather, evaluate, and synthesize information in meaningful and ethical ways, including the visual display of quantitative and qualitative data.

  4. Employ context-appropriate digital, visual, data and information literacy and numeracy skills to develop and/or evaluate design outcomes.

The Process

Research

The goal of my research was to collect appropriate data on consumerism for my data visualizations.

Through research studies and articles, I gathered statistics and research on manufacturing practices, waste and consumer culture. I found valuable information on worldwide views and actions towards consumption. I decided to focus on global manufacturing and consumer waste, which are the two key factors that are impacting the environment the most.

Selecting a research area

I first started by creating a concept or word map about consumerism. I researched each connection to consumerism and I was able to find a lot of information on how advertising and other methods of marketing impact consumers. However, I found that most of my research was tied to the environmental impact. I decided to research further into this and focused on the two subtopics that were impacting the environment the most; manufacturing practices, where I researched the environmental effects of the chemicals and materials used in the factory process, and wasted goods, where I researched the amount of produce that has been trashed.

Literature Review

Although there are many studies and research on each aspect of consumerism I am researching, there is less data on the overall impact the cycle has on the environment altogether. My research will fill the gap and connect each step in consumerism to find out where the problems lie globally.

Selecting methods of data collection

My data is collected through secondary research and the sources I have so far are listed at the end of the presentation. The research consists of articles, websites, research studies and statistics. Using secondary research helps increase the levels of my research validity.

Data analysis - Quantitative and Qualitative

Through my quantitative and qualitative research collected, I hope to identify and analyze patterns that relate to my aim and hypothesis. I hope to find reliable research and statistics that show that the environmental impact of consumerism can be decreased in many different ways, and who are the main contributors to this factor.

Reaching conclusions

Through my research, I found that a large percentage of recyclable waste is dumped and burned rather than recycled, and one-third of the food produced is trashed. Some lower-income cities lack proper waste management policies, leading to poorly disposed waste that results in a larger toll on the environment. However, low-income regions consume and purchase much fewer goods each year. Although high-income countries are only 16 percent of the population, they generate 34 percent of the worldâ€™s waste. This also affects the health and feed of the animals, as they require large amounts of produce. Around 1.7 billion people globally belong to the consumer class, accumulating high levels of non-essential products. To manufacture the product, large amounts of carbon monoxide are emitted in different stages of the process.

Data Visualization


I started off by creating concept sketches of my data

I chose a orange colour palette, as orange is associated to success, prestige, and wealth.

I then developed a contemporary moodboard with clear text and data.

Using Adobe Illustrator, I designed four vector artworks

Poster Series


Using the research content and the Data Visualizations, I created a series of posters to present my issue and research. To tie the series together, I experimented with different angles and created a version with a black background. I found that the table and bar graph worked better visually on a lighter background, so I kept two of the posters in white and two in black.

Final


Through this assignment, I became more confident in creating data visualization using Illustrator and InDesign. I am proud to be able to highlight the power of visualization to inform and persuade. Overall, I enjoyed working on this project and I am satisfied with my final layouts.
