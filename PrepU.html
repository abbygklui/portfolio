About this Project


PrepU is a web application that caters to the need for an easily accessible and effective job interview preparation program. It aims to help improve communication skills and advance career goals. This project was made possible through the assistance of the ADA Mentorship program, which is run by the Women in Tech Society (WITS). The program helps students develop technical projects with the guidance of professional developers. Our team comprises two product designers, Carol Chen and myself, two developers, Mengchan Zhao and Moneet Tiwana, and a project manager/apprentice, Anita So.

Problem

There is a lack of preparation tools available for students​ to improve their personal online interview performance.

Online interviews are becoming increasingly prevalent. COVID-19 has accelerated this growth. It has been difficult for university students to obtain personalized feedback from pre-recorded online interviews​ or to receive professional advice through mock interviews on digital platforms.

Online job interviews have increased by 49% in the last decade.1


60% of HR managers use or have used video interviewing software for assessment data in the hiring process.2

At the beginning of the pandemic, 85% of businesses switched to conduct online job interviews.3

"How can we offer a product that allows student job applicants to better their online interview performance?"

Our Approach


In order to best succeed in this goal, we decided to direct our project in the direction of a common way to prepare for interviews - mock interviews.

Flexible

Personalized and customizable features can be set prior to the mock interview

Convenient

Users can quickly practice for their interview anytime, anywhere

Practical

The application provides all forms of feedback - from punctuation to stylistic errors

Introducing... PrepU

With the use of voice and emotion recognition AI, our platform analyzes the user’s mock interview live by providing accurate, detailed feedback using machine algorithm.

Personalized Accessment


Users can customize their settings to focus on different features that affect interview performance the most. This includes emotion, body language, grammar, pauses, repetition and attire. Users can also turn on and off functions to focus on different forms of interviews.




Constant Feedback


Questions are asked throughout the interview, and generated feedback can be toggled to be shown live on the side to allow them to fix continuous mistakes while presenting.



Meaningful Insights


The user will be shown a summary of feedback based on the interview and features they wished to focus on. On the performance page, the user could compare and view their past interview performance from each mock interview, so they can better identify their frequent mistakes. We started by understanding what our user’s needs were.


  • I dove into platforms that were related to our project, including the software used for employers to analyze interview performance.
  • We then conducted a survey with a focus group of university students for feedback on current prioritized features and concepts they would find useful when practicing for an interview.

Competitive Analysis Key Takeaways

  • There is no accessible mock interview practice program that analyzes the voices and images of students.
  • Platforms that use machine learning to analyze performance during interviews are designed for employers.
  • Common features that affect hiring include grammar, tone, behaviour (particularly confidence), and facial movement.

User Survey Key Takeaways

  • Students want to have feedback given to the user along the way, then in-depth feedback after finishing the interview, so that they will know what mistakes they are making while presenting
  • Students want to have feedback on body language for better self expression
  • Students would prefer to have basic + premium subscription offerings over advert-based. Students believed that advertisements would be overwhelming and take away from the interview experience.

User Journey Key Takeaways

  • Create a Sense of Achievement and Progress: The interview experience should leave users with a feeling of accomplishment and advancement.
  • Prioritize User Comfort and Flexibility: Design the interface in a way that allows users to personalize their experience, ensuring they feel comfortable and have the flexibility to tailor it to their needs.
  • Serve the Needs of Different Users: The product should cater to both those who prepare for interviews well in advance and those who need last-minute preparation. It should provide valuable resources in the form of simple and advanced analytics, enabling users to continuously improve their interview skills.

3. Design

Solutions


Due to scheduling changes, we had limited time before moving the project into development. There were 2 key things we decided to focus on.

  • How can we design a product based on our user journey key takeaways?
  • What about our visual branding?

How can we keep the user comfortable and focused?

  • The mock interview page should place simplicity first with little interaction required from the user.
  • We can present simple feedback during the interview to help the user understand where their mistake is, then provide detailed feedback at the end

Create a Sense of Achievement and Progress


  • Throughout the mock interview, the AI offers both negative and positive feedback.
  • The UI features positive messages, such as displaying the number of questions answered and interviews completed. This uplifting interface motivates users to continue with the mock interviews.

Serve the Needs of Different Users


  • We split the features into 8 options for the user to be tested based on our key takeaways from the competitive analysis and user survey.
  • The type of data can also be selected and deselected on the dashboard

Establishing Professionalism through Branding


At this stage, we finalized how we wanted our design to look like. I aligned our branding and colour palette to establish our brand values: reliable, professional, scholarly.

Prioritize User Comfort and Flexibility


  • One thing I spent a while focusing on was the placement and type of button used to represent ending an interview early.
  • While I wanted the button's purpose to be clear, I did not want it to stand out too much compared to all the other features.

As red is commonly used to indicate "ending," I experimented with the slight use of red.

  • The live feedback on the right became a sidebar, so that users could choose if they would want to read their feedback during the interview or choose to keep the feedback hidden.
  • I added more features to help keep the user comfortable and less distracted throughout the interview.

My Reflection


Through this project, I learned the importance of considering the feasibility and technical constraints that would occur at the later stages of the project. From the start of the project, if we had asked ourselves questions such as “Are we going to be able to code certain features such as body language,” just as much as we had analyzed each aspect that would improve usability, we would have a better focus on critical features that would be developed.

Overall, this was a fun project, and it felt good to see what we had accomplished together in the past few months. In March 2021, our project was announced as the winner of this year's ADA Mentorship program, with the best overall technical implementation and user interface design.
